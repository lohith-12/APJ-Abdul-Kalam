About Sir C.V. Raman

Sir Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman, famously known as Sir C.V. Raman, was born on 7 November 1888 to Tamil Brahmin parents in Tiruchirapalli, Madras Presidency, British India. He won the Nobel Prize in Physics for discovering the famous ‘The Raman Effect’, also known as ‘Scattering of Light’, in 1930

Sir Raman is known for his significant contributions to the vibration and sound field, viz., musical instruments, diffraction, ultrasonic, x-ray diffraction, dielectrics, colloid optics, photo-electricity and the Raman Effect.

Sir C.V. Raman has won many notable awards, such as Fellow of the Royal Society (1924), Matteucci Medal (1928), Knight Bachelor (1930), Hughes Medal (1930), Nobel Prize in Physics (1930), Bharat Ratna (1954), Lenin Peace Prize (1957).
